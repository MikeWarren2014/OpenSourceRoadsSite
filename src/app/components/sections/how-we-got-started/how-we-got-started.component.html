HOW WE GOT STARTED

We came quite a ways since our humble beginnings in 2017.

Chris and Mike have, since before then, saw, over and over again, the ineptitude of local government to address an ongoing pothole crisis, here in Indianapolis. So much so, that our project Open Source Roads was borne of a simple idea: if the government won't fix the potholes, why don't we do it ourselves?

So, we went to our local Menards, and bought some cold patch, a tamper, some cones, and some shovels, brooms, and bucket with the money we raised from crowdfunding: about $100. Then, on April 4, we finally went to some potholes in my neighborhood and got to work. It was now or never.

We were, contrary to popular belief, poor college students then. We were, and still are, largely dependent on fundraising to fund our repairs. Hence, we strive to operate frugally, but quickly.

Chris and Mike have both, since then, found day careers. Our source of funding still remains self-funding and fundraisers.

While we are not professionals, we strive to learn more and more about pothole repair every day.
